My Top 3 Albums Of All Time

  1. '77 Live by Les Rallizes Denudes

    This album is a recording of band Les Rallizes Denudes live preformance at Tachikawa Hall, in Tokyo, 1977. It was all captured on one microphone, which resulted in a loud, drone-like distortion, reminiscent of what later becomes "noise rock".

  2. Floor by Boris

    Flood is the second studio album by Japanese experimental band Boris, released on December 15, 2000. It consists of a single 70-minute title-track that is broken into four movements. The long running album/song features various driven-peaks, and emotional lows, composing a masterful 70 minute track.

  3. In the Court of the Crimson King by King Crimson

    In the Court of the Crimson King (subtitled An Observation by King Crimson) is the debut studio album by English progressive rock band King Crimson, released on 10 October 1969, by Island Records.[3] Often regarded as the first true progressive rock album, it combined rock music influences with elements of jazz, classical, and symphonic music while describing the devastating effects of war and conflict, societal alienation and disillusionment, the perceived futility of human efforts against powerful, corrupt forces, unchecked authority and corrupt leadership.
